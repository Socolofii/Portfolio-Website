Ah Gong's Kopi Stall


A cooking game made for the H2 2025 Game Jame using Unity while collaborating with 4 other people in the span of 5 days.

I worked closely with 3 artists and one other programmer to design, as well as implement a short game with the theme of promoting Singapore culture within 5 days. The result is a cooking game where you have to serve customers while a timer runs down.

Description

Your uncle has gone away to take a break from managing his kopi stall. It is up to you to satisfy customers by serving them the right drinks in a timely manner. Listen to customer's requests and serve them the right drinks. Every day, you have to meet a quota which increases after each successful day. Try to last as many days as possible.





Role and Contributions

Role : Programmer


Contributions

- Prototyping as well as creating all the minigames

- Implementing the world space UI for the player to interact with during the gameplay

- Implementing the objectives (E.g. Timer before customers leave, Daily Quota)

- Implementing Audio to all everything listed before.

- Implemented the recipes for the drinks

Minigame : Pouring

Description

- Pour the liquid into the can below

- Move the top can left and right using arrows

- Make sure you don't spill the liquid

- You have three attempts to pour without spilling before you have to get another can, which wastes your precious time when serving customers.



Minigame : Timing Bar

Description

- Starts when adding ingredients to the drink

- Green bar moves up and down

- Tap the screen when the bar is in the green zone to successfully add the ingredient



Customer serving

Description

- When the player accepts a customer's order, a timer starts

- They have to serve the correct drink before the timer ends

- If the player gets the ingredients wrong, or they take too long, the customer gets angry and the player earns no money.

- If the player successfully serves the right drink, they gain money which contributes to the daily quota.



Midnight Rites


A 3D top-down hack and slash roguelike made for a final year project with 2 other programmers and 3 artists.

Worked with three artists and two other programmers to design the combat system and mechanics for this game. We were required to integrate AI into our workflow so we used it to generate 3D models and the artists refined and optimized the models to help the game run smoother. As programmers, we gave feedback to the artists on which models to focus on optimizing as the AI can sometimes generate too many vertices. This resulted in a much faster workflow where the artists can pump out assets faster than before.

Description

Take control of a character and defend the Singapore from otherworldly threats. Defeat enemies to gain currency and use it to buy powerful talismans and items. Manage your mana during combat and evade enemy attacks. Use the items and talismans you bought to become stronger and beat the boss.





Role and Contributions

Role : Programmer


Contributions

- Creating the inventory system to store items

- Implementing status effects that can be gained in combat

- Implementing items which can give status effects to help in combat.

- Creating the shop system where players can spend gold to buy items.

- Creating the item equip menu, where players can equip the talismans they bought.

Talisman Equip Screen

This is where the player can equip the talismans they bought

- Players can equip one of several offensive talismans.


Offensive talismans include

- Basic talisman : Fire a single shot forward

- Shotgun talisman : Fire a spread of weaker shots forward

- Bomb talisman : Fire an explosive talisman with weak splash damage

- Homing talisman : Fire a single weaker shot that homes in on enemies


Players can also equip talismans which give passive effects

- Players can equip one large talisman, which are talismans with two buffs and one debuff

- Players can equip two small talismans, which give the player a single buff



Shop Screen

This is where the player buy talismans and items

- There are two kinds of shops

- Ice cream cart shop which sells temporary or single use items

- Talisman shop which sells offensive and supporting talismans. There are two tabs, one of which contains small talismans and the other contains offensive and large talismans.



Risky Rain


A 3D third person roguelike made with three other programmers for a 3D game project in 2 weeks.

In this project, collaborating with peers was much easier as we are all programmers. We simply allocated tasks and made sure to ask for permission if we wanted to edit a file created by another peer. The result is an engaging game that has to be prototyped and implemented in 2 weeks.

Description

Choose a character and fight against hordes of enemies. Use the gold gained from defeating enemies to open chests and obtain items that make you stronger. Once ready, find the teleporter and defeat the boss to progress to the next level. But hurry, as enemies get stronger as time passes.





Role and Contributions

Role : Programmer


Contributions

- Creating and implementing the ai for the enemies

- Implementing core systems such as enemy spawning, currency, difficulty scaling and the objective

- Adding audio for the enemies and interactions with in game objects

Enemy : Beholder

Behaviour

- Floats towards the player when far away

- When near the player, orbits around them.

- When orbiting the player, periodically charges a laser and fires after a short time



Enemy : Mimic

Behaviour

- Disguises itself as a chest when spawning.

- When the player approaches or when hit, they let out a scream, stunning the player if they are close and waking up

- There is a short cooldown when they first spawn where they will not scream if the player is near

- When awake, they chase the player and do a melee attack when close enough



Enemy : Dragon

Behaviour

- A rare and powerful enemy.

- When out of melee range, periodically fires 5 fireballs towards the player

- Fireballs inflict a burn debuff if it hits the player, dealing damage over time

- When in melee range, it swipes twice with its wings, giving the player a bleed debuff

- The bleed debuff acts similar to burn, dealing damage over time



Time based difficulty

Behaviour

- As time passes, the difficulty level increases

- Chests which drop items cost more to open at higher difficulty levels

- Enemies drop more gold at higher difficulty levels

- Enemies get a stat boost based on the difficulty

- At higher difficulties, more enemies will spawn. Rarer enemies like the dragon will also have a higher chance of spawning per spawn instance
